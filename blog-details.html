trainning the young girls to make reusable pads

From the photo above, Our well trained staff participates in training the young girls to make reusable pads. In many parts of the country, girls and women face significant challenges when it comes to managing their menstrual cycles. They may not have access to disposable pads, or they may not be able to afford them. This can lead to girls missing school or work, as well as facing shame and stigma. Reusable pads provide a solution to these challenges by making menstrual products more accessible and affordable. Girls and women can make their own reusable pads using materials that are easily available, such as cotton, or they can purchase them from organizations that provide them at a low cost. The process of making reusable pads is simple, yet empowering. Girls and women learn how to make their own menstrual products, which can help to reduce the shame and stigma associated with menstruation. It also enables them to take control of their own menstrual health, and it can provide a sense of empowerment. Reusable pads are not only a sustainable option, but they also provide a practical solution for girls and women living in poverty or lack access to menstrual products. Furthermore, it can be a source of income for girls and women, as they can sell them to others in their communities.

influencing climate change

We are currently running a project of planting 10 million trees by 2025 to curb climate change. We have so far planted 870,000 trees. We are also in the process of creating an online service that helps green entrepreneurs and enterprises to scale up their activities. This is being done by creating a website that profiles these enterprises, connects them to prospective clients, leveraging social media platforms to advertise the work of the green enterprises, providing online support for registration and application for tax exemptions for these enterprises. We successfully carried out the training of 223 youth in Bukedea district on making of briquettes. We embarked on this activity with the aim of reducing charcoal burning in the society while providing the locals with incomes from the sale of the briquettes. People in Bukedea district have historically survived on charcoal ad the main source of fuel for cooking hence increased charcoal burning in the area. However, after Skilling the youth on making briquettes out of coffee husks and groundnut shells, we have been able to record significant reductions in tree burning. I am currently working with a team and with technical support from legal hub Uganda to create podcasts that will provide information to the youth on climate change, its effects and what the youth can do to create the positive change required.

waste management

Waste management is a critical issue in Uganda, as the country struggles to keep up with its rapidly growing population and urbanization. The problem is compounded by a lack of infrastructure and resources, as well as a lack of awareness among the population about the importance of proper waste managementOne of the main challenges in Uganda is the lack of proper collection and disposal systems for waste. In many urban areas, waste is often left on the streets or in open dumps, leading to pollution and health hazards. This is particularly problematic in densely populated areas, where the accumulation of waste can lead to the spread of disease and other public health issues. .Maraka foundation for youth development has parthenerd with different startups in the waste management to create and promote sustainable development by advocating, encouraging, and supporting vulnerable groups to carry out sustainable developmental projects.
