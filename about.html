who we are

Maraka Foundation for Development is a registered non-governmental Organization in Uganda that promotes sustainable development by advocating, encouraging, and supporting vulnerable groups to carry out sustainable developmental projects. We have managed to teach people living with HIV a number of life skills and business management skills. We are currently running a project of planting 10 million trees by 2025 to curb climate change. We have so far planted 870,000 trees. We are also in the process of creating an online service that helps green entrepreneurs and enterprises to scale up their activities. This is being done by creating a website that profiles these enterprises, connects them to prospective clients, leveraging social media platforms to advertise the work of the green enterprises, providing online support for registration and application for tax exemptions for these enterprises. We successfully carried out the training of 223 youth in Bukedea district on making of briquettes. We embarked on this activity with the aim of reducing charcoal burning in the society while providing the locals with incomes from the sale of the briquettes. People in Bukedea district have historically survived on charcoal ad the main source of fuel for cooking hence increased charcoal burning in the area. However, after Skilling the youth on making briquettes out of coffee husks and groundnut shells, we have been able to record significant reductions in tree burning. I am currently working with a team and with technical support from legal hub Uganda to create podcasts that will provide information to the youth on climate change, its effects and what the youth can do to create the positive change required.
